How to read this map. Each LGA is scored on four things: (1) how many recorded political-violence events have occurred there since 2020, (2) how many displaced people are currently living there, (3) how severely the OCHA Education Cluster rates the education situation for 2026, and (4) how many school-age children live more than 5 km from a working school. The scores are combined into one number; LGAs in the top third are red, the middle third orange, the bottom third yellow. Method detail: unweighted mean of four min-max-normalised indicators per LGA, tercile-classified. The OCHA JIAF Education layer replaces an earlier iMMAP 2019 school list whose Adamawa coverage had a state-wide data ceiling — see docs/adamawa_decision.md.

Sources: ACLED political-violence events (Apr 2026, CC BY-NC-SA 4.0); IOM DTM Round 50 (Oct 2025); OCHA JIAF Nigeria 2026 — Education sector PiN & Severity (2026 HNO); iMMAP / Education Sector school list (Jun 2019, context only); OCHA Nigeria COD-AB (current); UNFPA / NPC Nigeria population projections (2022); HeiGIT Global Education Accessibility Indicators (Feb 2026).
